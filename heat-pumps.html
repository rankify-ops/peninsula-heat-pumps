Why install a heat pump?

A smart, energy-efficient alternative

Using technology similar to a reverse-cycle air conditioner, a heat pump hot water system captures heat from the surrounding air and transfers it into your water supply — a smarter alternative to traditional electric or gas water heaters.

How it works

Heat pumps use refrigerant gas to absorb heat from the outside air. The gas is compressed (raising its temperature), then circulated through copper piping to transfer that heat into the water tank. The process repeats continuously, efficiently heating your water.

Why it's so efficient

Unlike traditional systems that generate heat with electric elements or gas burners, heat pumps simply move heat from the air into the water. This transfer process requires far less energy — making it a highly cost-effective solution for your home.

Solar-Friendly

Got rooftop solar? Use it twice as well

For homes with solar, a heat pump hot water system effectively becomes a solar storage solution. Heat your water during the day using free solar energy, then use that hot water all evening — squeezing far more value from every solar panel on your roof.

Energy efficient

Lower running costs
